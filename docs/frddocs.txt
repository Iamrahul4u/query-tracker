Definitions
Query refers to an inquiry before it becomes a qualified Lead (hereinafter referred to as a ‚ÄúLead‚Äù).
Query to Lead Definition & Flow
Query-related information is not to be entered into Salesforce and is to be handled entirely outside Salesforce. The allocation of Queries and the status buckets A to G, as defined herein, are to be applicable only to Queries.
A Query is to become a qualified Lead only once it reaches a minimum status of Bucket C or D. At this stage, the Query is to be entered into Salesforce by the CS person through the creation of a new object, i.e., a Lead (parent object).
The allocation of Queries and the movement of Queries across status buckets A to G are to be handled outside Salesforce, and no data relating to Query allocation or Query status changes is to be recorded or stored in Salesforce.
Even after a Query is entered into Salesforce as a Lead (upon reaching Bucket C or D), subsequent status movements of the Query to Buckets E or F are to continue to be managed outside Salesforce.
Accordingly, while the two functions‚Äî(a) Query allocation and status movement of Queries, and (b) Lead creation in Salesforce‚Äîare to be interdependent in terms of timing, the processes themselves are to remain independent. Query allocation and status management are to operate entirely outside Salesforce with no corresponding data capture in Salesforce, while the Lead record created in Salesforce is to function independently as the system-of-record entity.
Both functions outlined above are to be integrated with Gmail (to appear in and around Gmail) and must not require a separate sign-on or authentication beyond Gmail login.
1. Background & Problem Statement
The Client Servicing (CS) team currently allocates and manages Queries (received via phone calls, emails, and WhatsApp) through a fragmented workflow, largely coordinated via WhatsApp groups. This approach is to be replaced with a structured, organised, and foolproof mechanism with live visibility, by bringing Query allocation and tracking within the Gmail ecosystem (at least visually), given that each user operates on a Google Workspace email (Gmail) and spends the majority of their working time within it.
Queries that eventually qualify into Leads and are therefore required to be entered into Salesforce as Lead records are often delayed or skipped in the current process, resulting in poor visibility, incomplete CRM data, and reliance on individual discipline. To address this, a simple, intuitive, and natural interface and flow is to be created to enable the CS team to timely create and update Lead records in Salesforce via a single sign-on, without the need to separately log in to Salesforce.
